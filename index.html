RULES AND REGULATIONS


  1. The contest is open to legitimate undergraduate students of school year 2017-2018 from all colleges and universities in Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Graduates may still submit entries provided that the entry was made during their undergraduate years.

  2. Applicants may submit a copy of their entry in any of the following categories:

    1. Feature
    2. Documentary
    3. Music Video
    4. Experimental Video
    5. Pamana**

    The language of the film is open to any dialect. All entries must have English subtitles.
    All entries may be live action or animated regardless of category.

  3. A participant may submit more than one entry. However, an entry can only be submitted to one category.

  4. All entries must not exceed 20 minutes (including the opening and closing credits).

  5. Entries must be in MP4 format with a minimum quality of 720p. Other formats like Mini DVs, V8, NTSC AVI, and DVD format will not be accepted.

  6. Music videos must contain an original song from the filmmaker or must acquire the consent of the artist to use the song in the music video.

  7. Films that have been previously awarded in a major film competition in the national and international level will be disqualified. However, if an entry has been submitted to another competition but has not won an award at the time of submission in IUF 2018, the entry is still qualified.

  8. Entries that run over-time or contain pornographic elements such as nudity and the explicit depiction of sexual activity will also be disqualified.

  9. Participants must indicate if they have incurred help from professional production houses for the making of their film. Participants must specify the name of the production house and what specific work was done for the video.

  10. Deadline of submission of entries is on May 18, 2018. Entries submitted beyond the deadline will not be accepted.

**Additional rules for Pamana:
   A film can be submitted under the new category, if it meets any of the following criteria:

  • The film can focus or be situated in (a) certain location/s in the Philippines.
  • The film can be centralized on a certain Filipino tradition / practice / ritual / culture.
  • The plot can be a fictionalized significant event in PH history.
  • The film can be in another dialect besides Tagalog.
  • The film is either a feature or a documentary.

JUDGING PROCESS


1. All entries will be filtered by the organizers along with selected judges to determine the entries which will qualify for the awards and be shown on the screening. The IUF Selection Committee reserves the right to award the finalist status to any entry.
2. Decisions of the judges are final with respect to all matters relating to the IUF 2018 film competition. The judges reserve the right to not give any awards in a category should no entry merit it.
3. In the event that only one entry will qualify in a category, the entry will automatically be declared as the winner; however, the participant will only receive half of the cash prize.
4. Finalists will be notified through text message and e-mail, as well as our Facebook page, and will be given 2 complimentary festival passes. The complimentary passes will be given on the day of the event if and only if the finalists have confirmed attendance.
